The Original Takeaway

2013, Hayden Stainsby

I am currently working towards my PhD in Mathematics at the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona. My research is based on the Montes Algorithm, the output of which I'm working with to more efficiently compute integral bases of number fields.

For more information on the current state of what's being done with the Montes Algorithm, our latest article is a good place to start.

As a computer scientist turned mathematician, my background in mathematical fundamentals isn't as strong as it could be. However (aside from thoroughly enjoying mathematics), I've found that applying my CS experience to other fields I'm working in can be very advantageous.

My more personal research interests lie in cryptography - and computer security in general - and in the learning of mathematics.

Pre-prints

Complexity of OM Factorizations of Polynomials Over Local Fields

Jens-Dietrich Bauch, Enric Nart, Hayden D. Stainsby

Abstract: Let $k$ be a locally compact complete field with respect to a discrete valuation $v$. Let $\mathcal{O}$ be the valuation ring, $\mathfrak{m}$ the maximal ideal and $F(x)\in\mathcal{O}[x]$ a monic separable polynomial of degree $n$. Let $\delta=v(\operatorname{Disc}(F))$. The Montes algorithm computes an OM factorization of $F$. The single-factor lifting algorithm derives from this data a factorization of $F(\md \mathfrak{m}^{\nu})$, for a prescribed precision $\nu$. In this paper we find a new estimate for the complexity of the Montes algorithm, leading to an estimation of $O(n^{2+\epsilon}+n^{1+\epsilon}\delta^{2+\epsilon}+n^2\nu^{1+\epsilon})$ word operations for the complexity of the computation of a factorization of $F(\md \mathfrak{m}^{\nu})$, assuming that the residue field of $k$ is small.
